# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

### Touchpoints
- **Product Manager**: Align on acceptance criteria and feature priorities
- **Project Manager**: Coordinate sprint planning and dependency management
- **UX/UI Designer**: Collaborate on design specifications and usability
- **Quality Analyst**: Review test plans and acceptance criteria
- **Release Manager**: Coordinate deployment and rollback procedures

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

### Touchpoints
- **Project Manager**: Align on priorities, timeline, and resource needs
- **Developers**: Define acceptance criteria and success metrics
- **UX/UI Designer**: Research user needs and validate design direction
- **Stakeholders**: Present roadmap, gather input, and communicate value
- **Quality Analyst**: Define acceptance test strategy

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

### Touchpoints
- **Product Manager**: Align on priorities and roadmap
- **Scrum Master**: Coordinate sprint ceremonies and team capacity
- **Developers**: Track progress, identify blockers, facilitate unblocking
- **Release Manager**: Coordinate release planning and deployment windows
- **Stakeholders**: Provide regular status updates and escalate risks

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile ceremonies, remove blockers, and foster a culture of continuous improvement. They support the team in adhering to agile practices and help optimize workflow efficiency.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint planning, daily standups, reviews, and retrospectives
- Help identify and remove impediments blocking team progress
- Coach the team on agile practices and process improvements
- Track sprint metrics (velocity, burndown) and share insights
- Support the Project Manager in maintaining team capacity and focus

### Goals
- Enable the team to maintain consistent velocity and delivery cadence
- Foster psychological safety and continuous improvement
- Minimize context switching and distractions
- Ensure agile ceremonies remain focused and time-boxed

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint ceremonies
- Retrospective action item tracking
- Metrics and velocity reports
- One-on-ones with team members to understand blockers

### Touchpoints
- **Project Manager**: Collaborate on sprint planning and capacity management
- **Developers**: Remove technical blockers and support team flow
- **Product Manager**: Help prioritize backlog and manage scope creep
- **Release Manager**: Coordinate release readiness and deployment timing

---

## UX/UI Designer

### Role Summary
UX/UI Designers own the user experience and visual design of the product. They conduct user research, create wireframes and prototypes, and validate usability with customers and stakeholders.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Define information architecture and interaction patterns
- Ensure accessibility standards are met (WCAG, keyboard navigation, screen readers)
- Collaborate with developers on implementation details
- Gather feedback and iterate on designs based on user insights

### Goals
- Deliver user-centered, intuitive solutions
- Reduce support tickets and user confusion through clear design
- Ensure design consistency across the product
- Balance user needs with business constraints

### Typical Communication
- Design critiques and stakeholder reviews
- Usability testing sessions and findings
- Design specifications and component documentation
- Design handoff to development team

### Touchpoints
- **Product Manager**: Validate user needs and prioritize design initiatives
- **Developers**: Provide specifications and collaborate on feasibility
- **Quality Analyst**: Define acceptance criteria for usability and accessibility
- **Stakeholders**: Present design concepts and gather feedback
- **UX Researcher** (if separate role): Conduct and share user research insights

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers own the release process, ensuring smooth and safe deployments to production. They coordinate with engineering, QA, and operations to minimize risk and maintain service reliability.

### Responsibilities
- Own the release checklist and deployment procedures
- Coordinate release timing with product and engineering teams
- Prepare and communicate release notes
- Manage rollback and incident response procedures
- Verify successful deployments and post-release health checks
- Track release metrics and document lessons learned

### Goals
- Ensure reliable, repeatable releases with minimal downtime
- Reduce deployment risk and incident frequency
- Maintain clear communication with stakeholders during releases
- Continuously improve release processes

### Typical Communication
- Release planning meetings with PM, engineering, and QA
- Release notes and deployment announcements
- Post-deployment verification reports
- Incident escalation and rollback coordination

### Touchpoints
- **Project Manager**: Coordinate release windows and stakeholder communication
- **Developers**: Verify code readiness and coordinate deployment
- **Quality Analyst**: Confirm acceptance testing complete; coordinate smoke tests
- **Operations/DevOps**: Manage deployment pipeline and infrastructure
- **Stakeholders**: Announce releases and manage expectations during deployment windows

---

## Quality Analyst (QA)

### Role Summary
Quality Analysts specialize in manual and exploratory testing, acceptance test writing, and verification of business requirements. They partner with developers and product managers to ensure deliverables meet quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Write and execute acceptance test cases based on acceptance criteria
- Perform manual and exploratory testing to identify edge cases
- Verify bug fixes and validate improvements
- Define and maintain test plans and test data
- Partner with developers on continuous testing and automation strategies
- Document test results and track defect lifecycle

### Goals
- Catch defects early and prevent low-quality releases
- Ensure acceptance criteria are thoroughly validated
- Support faster feedback cycles through clear testing insights
- Enable developers to focus on automation and quality engineering

### Typical Communication
- Acceptance test specifications and execution reports
- Defect reporting with detailed reproduction steps
- Test coverage and quality metrics dashboards
- Collaboration during sprint planning on testability

### Touchpoints
- **Product Manager**: Clarify acceptance criteria and business requirements
- **Developers**: Report defects, discuss edge cases, support test automation
- **Release Manager**: Conduct pre-release smoke tests and sign-off verification
- **Project Manager**: Report test progress and quality metrics
- **UX/UI Designer**: Validate usability and accessibility requirements

---

## Stakeholder

### Role Summary
Stakeholders include business leaders, external partners, and key business function owners who have vested interest in project outcomes. They provide strategic input, approve milestones, and help remove organizational blockers.

### Responsibilities
- Provide business context and strategic alignment for project goals
- Review and approve project charter and success metrics
- Provide feedback on deliverables and validate business value
- Help escalate and remove organizational blockers
- Communicate project value and outcomes to broader organization

### Goals
- Ensure project delivers measurable business value
- Maintain organizational alignment and executive visibility
- Remove obstacles that prevent team success
- Build trust and confidence through transparent communication

### Typical Communication
- Monthly stakeholder status updates and reviews
- Milestone approval meetings
- Ad-hoc escalation and blocker resolution
- Post-project retrospectives and value validation

### Touchpoints
- **Project Manager**: Receive regular status updates and escalations
- **Product Manager**: Review roadmap and validate business priorities
- **Scrum Master**: Invited to key ceremonies for visibility and feedback
- **Release Manager**: Informed of release schedule and business impact

## How these personas are used in the exercise

- Use these persona definitions to frame scenarios and sample interactions in the Skills Exercise.
- Each persona can be used as a persona prompt for Copilot Spaces to shape role-specific guidance.
- When defining new processes or workflows, explicitly document how each persona interacts with the process.
- Use touchpoints to identify communication needs and potential handoff points in your project workflows.
